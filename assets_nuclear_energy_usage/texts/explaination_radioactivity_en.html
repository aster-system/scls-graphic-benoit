The radioactivity

------------------------------------------------------ In this part, we will explain in the simpliest way possible the main problem
with the nuclear energy : the radioactivity. ------------------------------------------------------ Some nucleus are unstable in the time, while some others, too heavies, or
unbalanced, are unstable in the time. The unstables nucleus are radioactives.
To become stable again, they experience what it is called a desintegration,
throwing away some particules, and also some waves, which represent the
unfamous radioactivity. Some of thoses radiations are easily stopable with
water, when some others need severals cms of lead. ------------------------------------------------------ Thoses radiations are "ionizing" : they can transform some atoms in ions.
However, thoses transformations can affect the atoms in an human body. If
something like that happens, some health risks can occurs to the exposed
person. Two of the possibles risks are the spawning of a cancer, of an
heavy radiation syndrome (which is considered as one of the most painful
death possible). ------------------------------------------------------ After a nuclear fission, it is heavily probable that the two newly created
atoms are radioactives. They are called the "final waste". The can also
make the materials around them radiaoctives, but least radioactives than
they are. Thoses materials are classed according to their radioactivity
level.
