The energy from an atom

------------------------------------------------------ In this part, we will explain in the simpliest way possible how littles
atoms can produce so much energy. ------------------------------------------------------ If a nucleus of an atom is too heavy, the repulsion between each protons
become too strong, and the nucleus become unstable. Some nucleus are so
heavy than they can be broken in two pieces if they collide with a
neutron. This phenomenon is called the "induced nuclear fission". Thoses
nucleus are called "fissiles nucleus". ------------------------------------------------------ The only fissile nucleus that can be found in the nature is the uranium
235. A collision with a neutron has a strong chance to make a fission
of the nucleus, which is broken in two smaller nucleus. Thoses two
pieces are repulsed, and gain so a lot of cinetic energy. This energy
are converted in thermal energy in a certain amount of time, which allows
power plants to work. Some others elements can make the same phenomenon :
plutonium 239, thorium 227... ------------------------------------------------------ During the fission, the nucleus can expluse some neutrons. If thoses
neutrons collides with some others nucleus, so the phenomenon is
repeated. This repetition is called "the chain reaction". If the needed
conditions are united for this reaction, the fissile matter bloc is in its
"critical mass".
