The matter basis

------------------------------------------------------ In this part, we will explain in the simpliest way possible what is the basis
of the matter, and from where the energy can come. ------------------------------------------------------ Firstly, it is important to say that all the matter which around us is
composed of atoms. Thoses atoms are composed of a central part called the
nucleus of the atom. This nucleus is composed of composites particules
called the protons and the neutrons. ------------------------------------------------------ The element which the nucleus is part of is defined by its number of protons.
According to the number of neutrons in it, the atom is called an isotop,
called by its number of neutrons in the nucleus. For exemple, a carbon atom
which has 14 neutrons is called "carbon 14". ------------------------------------------------------ Even if protons has a positive charge, they do not repulse themselves.
Indeed, protons, and also neutrons, are made of tinier particules called
quarks. Thoses particules are pasted with each others with a specific force
called the "strong force". This force allows the linking between all the
quarks, and so between the protons and the neutrons.
